First things first, I was an exchange student in Hungary for a year, 6 out of those 12 months I decided to dive into a software development company (August 2014 - June 2015). You may be wondering why? Well truth is I found myself really interested in learning how to create websites, applications and basically anything that had code in it. I realised I was spending about 4 hours every day learning how to code and I thought "Maybe if I could invest these 4 hours with someone that has already learned what I'm trying to learn I could learn faster”.

The whole experience was really good, I had the chance to “live” like a programmer for 6 months, funny thing is I tried to learn how to create applications the same way I learned Hungarian (Through experience) And after two weeks trying it out I concluded that learning through experience is way harder if you don’t have a base from where you can hold on to.

I took a couple courses on Java programming through lynda.com, after feeling confident I could start building something I started to put the little knowledge I had to practice. I made a little app in which you could enter and set up a daily morning routine. The user was able to set a profile picture and change their name/password in the settings panel.

From January 2014 till June 2014 I worked in a Mexican startup called Zione.

It all started when my father told me he had a friend who was creating a business and he needed a graphic designer. By the time I had basic photoshop skills.

I decided to take my chances and try it out, maybe he would like what I could do. In the interview I was asked to create multiple images for the design of a website. I did my best and got the job done. The whole motivation behind this job opportunity was to get a chance to learn from someone who already built a whole platform by himself.

Unfortunately I wasn't able to learn much due to the fact that it was a part time job and all the hours spent in the office were meant to be 100% efficient.I had a good time getting to know the company's culture and I designed many visual interfaces. This was my second professional experience, I learned on the go. Every day I learned something different, I realised that sometimes it's a good idea to take a break when working because you can get "Cycled" in a single idea or design. I recently found that that is also true when programming. I also learned how to manage my time effectively to deliver what I was asked for. Overall I feel like that was a really good professional experience.


On christmas of 2013, I worked as an animator. This was my first professional experience ever. I would dress up as an Elf, Rudolf and only twice I got the chance to be Santa Claus. We went to all sort of events around Monterrey, my hometown.

It was hard to catch up with all the other experienced animators, they all knew the right things to say on several situations and none of them felt nervous when facing a big crowd.

I remember the time I got the chance to represent Santa Claus in a kindergarden. I have to say, it was hard to fake my voice and talk in front of a 100 people room, but as time went on it became easier and easier to talk in front of a big audience.


Things I learned:

  1. I developed a lot my soft skills.
  2. I developed my creativity to find the right words in a short amount of time.
  3. I understood how much time and effort it takes to make some money.
