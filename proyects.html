Lately I’ve been learning by myself how to code in Swift to start building apps. It has been tough due to the fact that code used in applications is way different than code used to solve math problems for example.

I’ve always had a lot of ideas, and the problem is that I want to make them all a reality. After thinking back and forth what my next steps of actions should be in order for me to make them all a reality I came up with a couple solutions I’m working on currently.

Solution number 1: Find people that would like to contribute with those ideas and create one by one the apps I want to build. (In the week of Nov 2nd till Nov 6th I’ll be participating in my first Hackathon ever and I found a group of people that are also interested in building apps so we’ll see how it goes)

Solution number 2: Get good at creating prototypes. Do a market research and be 100% sure there are people that would be willing to buy the product. Find people that would be willing to invest in one of the ideas. Get funded. And last but definitely not least hire someone to develop the app I want to create.

I’ll leave some updates on the findings I have about this topic, which way works better etc.

A few months ago (August 2015) I joined a school group called “Program for leader entrepreneurs” at my school. It’s all about developing a little bit more soft skills, business skills, and self-knowledge.

It’s a blessing to get to know people that have more less the same mindset. Those kind of people you meet and you feel like you’ve known them for a lifetime. And that opportunity I’ve acquired while being in this group.

One of the goals of this program is for us to have a business idea that would satisfy a necessity. We made teams and my team consists of 8 persons. We came up with a pretty good idea and we’ve been working on developing the front end of what will probably in the future be a platform. (Using Invision Software) We’re meeting about twice every week to work on this project.

I think it’s a good opportunity to start understanding how building a business looks like inside out, from Zero to 100. I always wondered what it would take for someone to build a platform and that’s exactly what I’m figuring out right now.

Roborregos is a student group in which we use our skills to create a robot that will then be put to the test in multiple events. We just built a team, we’re 4 and we’ll be working on the robot over the next couple weeks.

I see this as an opportunity to learn which other things code can do, and how it can actually interact with the real world.
